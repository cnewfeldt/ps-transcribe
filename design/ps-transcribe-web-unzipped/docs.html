Getting started

Install, grant two permissions, point PS Transcribe at an Obsidian folder, and start recording. Five minutes.

installInstall

Download the latest signed DMG from GitHub Releases. Drag PS Transcribe.app into /Applications. On first launch, Gatekeeper will verify the Developer ID signature.

Auto-updates are handled by Sparkle — future releases appear as a quiet banner in the app, nothing forced.

Requirements macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later. Apple Silicon recommended; Intel builds run noticeably slower during diarization.

permissionsGrant permissions

Two prompts, both required:

  1. Microphone — for capturing your side of the call.
  2. Screen & System Audio Recording — for capturing the other side via ScreenCaptureKit. PS Transcribe does not record your screen; it uses the system-audio tap exposed by the same API.

If you miss a prompt, re-open it in System Settings → Privacy & Security.

vaultPoint at an Obsidian vault

Open Preferences → Vault and pick the folder where transcripts should land. A typical setup:

Vault path:      ~/Documents/Obsidian/Work
Meetings path:   Meetings/{{year}}
Memos path:      Memos/{{year}}-{{month}}
Filename:        {{date}} — {{title}}.md

Template tokens are resolved at save time. Frontmatter is written automatically — see the Frontmatter schema reference.

recordRecord something

Press ⌘ R anywhere in the app. A red dot appears in the sidebar. PS Transcribe starts two streams: your mic, and the system-audio tap for whoever else is in the call.

While recording, the transcript renders as it goes — not polished yet, just enough to follow along. When you press ⌘ . to stop, the app runs voice-activity detection, final speech recognition, and speaker diarization. Usually under a minute for a 30-minute call on Apple Silicon.

What "on-device" actually means Parakeet-TDT, Silero VAD, and the diarization clusterer all run locally via FluidAudio. No API keys. No network calls during transcription. You can verify with Little Snitch or the Network tab in Activity Monitor.

notionSend to Notion (optional)

If you've connected a Notion integration in Preferences → Integrations, each session offers a "Send to Notion" toggle before you stop. The transcript posts as a new database page with frontmatter mapped to properties.

Leave it off entirely and PS Transcribe never talks to Notion — the integration stays dormant.

Keyboard shortcuts

PS Transcribe is built for the keyboard. Four shortcuts cover 90% of the app; the rest are for speaker management and navigation.

Recording

⌘R
Start a meetingCaptures mic + system audio in parallel.
⌘⇧R
Start a quick memoMic only. Single-speaker. No diarization pass.
⌘.
Stop and saveRuns VAD, final recognition, and diarization.
⌘⇧.
Stop without savingDiscards the session. Prompts for confirmation.
Space
Pause / resumeOnly available during a recording.

Transcript

⌘E
Rename speakerClick a bubble, then ⌘E. Every bubble from that speaker updates.
⌘F
Find in sessionScoped to the currently-open transcript.
⌘K
Quick switcherFuzzy jump to any session in the library.
⌘J
Jump to liveScrolls to the current recording position.

Layout

⌘⇧S
Toggle sidebarHides the library column.
⌘⇧I
Toggle inspectorHides the session-metadata column.
⌘0
Reset zoomReturns transcript text to default size.
Customize All shortcuts can be rebound in Preferences → Keyboard. Your overrides are written to ~/Library/Application Support/PS Transcribe/shortcuts.json.
